Rube Goldberg Machine

As a part of the DFM class ME 270, I, along with 2 others, designed a 20 step mechanism which started with a marble drop and ended by turning off a light switch. I designed mechanical actions using Fusion360 and manufactured the mechanisms using a 3D printer.

Our project included an open circuit which was closed when a metal marble was launched from a lever. Wire wrapped around a nail created an electromagnet which pulled another marble to activated another series of mechanical steps.

Combined a pulley system with a switch to activated servo motors which were controlled by an Arduino board

Self-Leveling Bike Cup Holder

In the introductory Computer-Aided Design course, we were tasked design a product that solved any problem.

In my group of 4, we designed and optimized a gimbal bike cup holder for mass production through injection molding. Our team outlined sourcing, manufacturing, and distribution plans to bring the design to market
